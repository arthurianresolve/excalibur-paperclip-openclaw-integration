# Banter → Flirt Playbook

## Core mindset
- Create **positive tension**: mix uncertainty with excitement so the exchange stays bright and engaging.
- Signal interest without neediness: playful contradictions + warm tone.
- Keep it light; avoid heavy, needy, or blunt lines.
- Read the room; mirror energy and back off when it doesn’t land.

## Two routes

### 1) Direct flirtation (exaggerated rejection)
Use ridiculous reasons to “reject” while your tone signals interest.
- Example: “I only date people who can beat me at Mario Kart… so you’re in trouble.”
- Example: “I promised myself I’d never date troublemakers. You’re a walking red flag.”

### 2) Indirect flirtation (reverse suggestion)
Say it “won’t work,” which plants the thought while staying playful.
- Example: “We could never survive a fake Vegas wedding. We’d be too competitive.”
- Example: “We should absolutely *not* plan a ridiculous date idea. That would be dangerous.”

## Banter templates (adapt to context)
- Clever challenge: “If I make you laugh, you owe me coffee. If I fail, I still owe you coffee.”
- Witty accusation: “You’re only here for the late‑night snack raids, right?”
- Smart forecast: “Forecast says laughter, then a date. Sounds accurate.”
- Playful bargain: “You share one embarrassing story, I’ll share one. Deal?”
- Flirtatious dare: “Text the most charming person you met today… maybe that’s me.”
- Quirky confession: “Already planned our chocolate heist. You in?”

## Texting & timing
- Keep replies **short, confident, and light**.
- Leave a beat after a punchline so it lands.
- Open by referencing the last exchange, then add a cheeky hook.
- Use emojis sparingly to mark playfulness.

## Delivery checklist
- Smile / signal playfulness in tone.
- Maintain eye contact (or a warm tone in text).
- Match their pace before escalating.
- If a line doesn’t land, soften and pivot.
